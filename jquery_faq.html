FAQ about US National Parks

Who is the director of the National Park Service?
The director of the National Park System is Charles F. "Chuck" Sams III
How many employees are in the National Park System?
There are approximately 20,000 employees in the National Park System.
How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.
Which was the nation's first National Park?
The nation's first National Park was Yellowstone National Park in Wyoming established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872
How many areas are part of the National Park System?
he system includes 423 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
What is the Largest National park site?
The largest National Park is the Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve in Alaska at 13.2 million acres
What is the smallest National park site?
The smallest National Park is the Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial in Pennsylvania at .02 acres.
How many people visit the National Parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2021: 297,115,406.
What was the most visited National Park site in 2021?
The most visited park site in 2021 was the Blue Ridge Parkway with 15.9 million visitors which goes from The Great Smoky Mountain National Park in Tennessee to Shenandoah National Park in Virginia.
What government agency oversees the National Park System?
The National Park System is a bureau of the Department of the Interior.

    Yellowstone

    • YellowStone was the first National Park established in March 1, 1872
    • Yellowstone is in the state of Wyoming
    • Yellowstone is larger than the states of Rhode Island and Delaware combined.
    • Yellowstone is home to more than 500 active geysers which is over half the world's geysers.

    Grand Canyon

    • The age of the Grand Canyon varies from 6 million to 70 million years ago.
    • The FAA was created due to two planes colliding while sightseeing over the Grand Canyon
    • Paul Bunyan did not create the Grand Canyon
    • There is a town in the Grand Canyon with a population of 208. Supai Village is the most remote community in the continental United States.

    Big Bend

    • Big Bend was made a national park on June 20, 1935
    • The name Big Bend comes from a large bend in the Rio Grande River along the park boundary.
    • In 2012, the park was named as an International Dark Sky Park. Big Bend National Park has the darkest measured skies in the lower 48 states, and star gazing here is obviously superb.
    • The large range of elevations in the park contribute to its biological diversity. There are 56 species of reptiles, 75 species of mammals, more than 450 species of birds, along with at least 1,200 species of plants.
